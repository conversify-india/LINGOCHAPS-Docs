# 🚀 Lingo Chaps TMS: New Joiner Orientation Guide

Welcome to the team! We have upgraded our operations from manual spreadsheets to a centralized **Translation Management System (TMS)** built inside Zoho Projects. This system ensures we deliver high-quality work, track our profits, and keep our clients happy with zero manual effort.

---

## 1. What have we built?
We have created a "Project Engine." Instead of manually typing out tasks for every new translation project, we now use a **Standard TEP Template** (Translation, Editing, Proofreading). This template automatically sets deadlines and dependencies for every step of the process.

## 2. Why did we build it?
*   **Speed:** We can now set up a complex project in 30 seconds.
*   **Financial Clarity:** We track exactly how much we pay Upwork freelancers vs. how much the client pays us, right inside the project dashboard.
*   **Professionalism:** Our clients receive automated, beautifully formatted delivery emails the moment we finish a task.
*   **Scalability:** This system allows us to handle 50 projects as easily as 5 projects.

---

## 3. How to use it (Your Daily Workflow)

### Step A: Starting a Project
When a new client order comes in:
1.  Create a **New Project**.
2.  Choose the **Standard TEP Template**. 
3.  **Magic:** Three tasks appear instantly: `01. Translation`, `02. Editing`, and `03. Final QC`.

### Step B: Managing Upwork Freelancers
For every task, you must fill in three pieces of data:
1.  **Source & Target Language:** Select from the dropdown.
2.  **Upwork Contract URL:** Paste the link to the freelancer's contract so we can find them later.
3.  **Upwork Freelancer Cost:** Enter what we are paying them. Zoho will automatically calculate our profit margin for that task.

### Step C: The "One-Click" Delivery
When the Final QC is done and the files are uploaded:
1.  Change the Task Status to **`Awaiting Client Approval`**.
2.  **That’s it.** Zoho will automatically send a professional email to the client with the delivery notes. You don't need to write a manual email.

### Step D: Handling Feedback (Revision Requests)
If a client wants changes, do **not** use email. 
1.  Log the request and select the **Revision Type** (e.g., Grammar, Accuracy, or Style).
2.  Assign it back to the freelancer. This allows us to track which freelancers are making too many mistakes!

---

## 4. How it helps our "Upwork & Client Connection"
*   **Upwork Connection:** By keeping the Upwork URL and costs inside Zoho, we never lose track of who did what or how much we spent. If a client complains 6 months later, we can find the exact Upwork contract in 2 seconds.
*   **Client Connection:** Clients see us as a high-end agency. They get instant notifications, a professional portal to upload revisions, and clear status updates. It builds **Trust**, which leads to repeat business.

---

### 💡 Pro-Tip for New Joiners:
"The system only works if the data is accurate. Always ensure the **Upwork Cost** and **Statuses** are updated in real-time. If the status isn't updated, the client doesn't get their work!"
