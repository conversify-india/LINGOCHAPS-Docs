Intake & Global Communications

Mission Critical: Intake Protocols

Every project begins here — follow these protocols to ensure nothing is missed before work begins.

CRITICAL REQUIREMENT: INSTANT REPLY

"YouTube project clients communicate via WhatsApp and Email — immediate responses are required at all times."

1. Mandatory OST, Timezone, ETA, quantity of work Check

Verify the OST quantity,Timezone, ETA, quantity of work immediately. Your ability to hit the ETA is directly tied to the text quantity of the video or timezone of the resource. Use the following decision tree:

Scenario A: Low OST, manageable ETA (e.g., 3–4 hours), or availability in the actual required timezone.

Scenario B: High OST, Insufficient ETA, or Timezone Conflict, Negotiate a realistic ETA based on quantity of work before starting execution.

Critical OST Execution Tips

  • Mandatory English Conversion: In ALL cases, converting OST to English text is a hard requirement. (Website tool link will be provided).
  • Language Protocols: If OST is English, convert directly. If OST is in another language, the assigned resource will handle translation.
  • Screenshot (SS) Requirement: High-quality screenshots of the OST are MANDATORY to facilitate the conversion process. No exceptions.

2. Resource Selection & Escalation

Be fully aware of resource availability across different timezones before accepting the task to ensure proper assignment and timely completion. Once a task is accepted, initiate immediate resource sourcing following this hierarchy:

Step 1: Internal Search

Consult the Language List Excel Sheet and contact linguists via WhatsApp/Upwork immediately.

Step 2: Escalation

If no response from current resources, consult a Senior Manager to post a new job on Upwork.

3. Audio Quality & Audibility Assessment

Before accepting the work, perform a quick audio check. If any segment is not audible properly or has quality issues, follow this escalation protocol:

Step A: Translator Review

Give the audio to a specialized translator for a professional check regarding audibility and context.

Step B: Client Consultation

Inform the client about the quality issue and suggest possible solutions. The next steps will depend on the client's decision.

Virtual Room: Stay connected via Google Meet at all times (muted/video off) for instant collaboration.
Resource Splitting: Be prepared to divide high-quantity tasks across multiple linguists to protect deadlines.

We primarily manage two types of clients: (Client C1) and (Client C2). Their distinct intake and operational requirements are detailed below.

Client C1 Protocol

Tasks arrive via Email with comprehensive documentation (Timestamps, ETA, and specific instructions).

  • Foreign Language Check: If a foreign language is detected in the video, inform the client and get confirmation whether the task needs to be done.
  • Verify yourself OST and ETA details upon receipt.
  • Comments:Confirm timestamps and ETA before execution, and timestamps to work on can be requested based on team capacity and timezone availability.

GLOSSARY TIP: "Foreign Language" refers to any language detected in the video apart from the specific task languages. This includes background noise, music, or speech in another language (e.g., Hindi or Arabic appearing in a Spanish-to-English task).

Client C2 Protocol

Communication is via WhatsApp or Email.

  • Triad Check: Immediately verify Video, OST, and Deadline.
  • ETA Assessment: If any foreign language is detected check if translation can be done within the given ETA. If not, request a time limit extension to get the job done correctly.
  • Negotiation: Request an extension only if criteria (e.g., resource availability, tight time limits) are technically impossible.

Timezone Alignment Check

The Project Manager or Associate should have a clear understanding of different time zones to ensure effective task management when work is assigned by the client. This helps maintain a smooth workflow and ensures timely delivery.

  • Align resources according to the client’s deadline.
  • Be aware of time zone differences based on the language resources requested by the client.
  • Assess whether the task can be completed within the given ETA, whether an extension should be requested to ensure proper delivery of the file, or whether the task should be declined due to a significant time zone gap of 4–9 hours.

Whatsapp Forwarding Protocol

All client communications must be instantly mirrored to the relevant internal whatsapp execution groups.

REQUIRED MESSAGES TO FORWARD:

  • Proceed: Forward the client’s confirmation or go-ahead message.
  • Video Link: Share the direct/raw video links provided by the client.

Mandatory Project Safeguards

Mandatory Backup & Sync Protocol

Always maintain a local backup of the live template. To avoid loss of work and progress (in case translator intentionally deletes the file.), you must download and save the template locally at regular intervals throughout the project. Regardless of video length (3 or 5 mins), backups are mandatory for all projects.

Isolated Intake

Never share client Drive links with translators. Download the video locally first, then share that link/file with the resource.

Secure Delivery

Never deliver internal template links to the client. Migrate work into the client's master template first, then perform the final delivery.

Storage Cleanup Protocol

To optimize disk space, local videos and backups can be deleted 24 hours after successful delivery to the client. Always maintain the 1-day buffer before clearing local files.

Template Structure & Setup

Required Data Fields

  • Header Info: Video Title, Link, Target Language, and Profile Name.
  • Video Summary: A concise 2-3 line overview of the content.
  • Metadata: Translated Video Description and Thumbnail Text.
  • Additional Comments: Notes on specific terminology or nuances.

Execution Rules

1. Timestamping: Must follow (HH:MM:SS) format per minute.

2. Cultural Adaptation: Translate idioms/slang for context, not literally. Provide Notes in brackets if needed(depending on project).

3. Speaker ID: Clearly identify Speaker A, B, etc., as they speak.

4. Manual Only: Absolutely NO Machine Translation allowed.

Quality Assurance

Review Protocols & Quality Control

Linguist Execution Protocol

ZERO TOLERANCE: Machine Translation (Google/AI) is strictly prohibited. Manual, human-crafted translation is the only acceptable standard.

Core Requirements

  • Online Only: Work directly on the Google Doc link. Offline files are not acceptable.
  • Video Summary: Provide a concise 2-3 line summary explaining the video content.
  • Comment Resolution: Actively resolve all internal queries/comments added to the file.
  • Verbatim Accuracy: Provide word-for-word translation. No masking or hiding of swear words or abusive language.

Technical Handling

  • Inaudible Parts: Mention timestamps for inaudible or cut speech (e.g., [00:03:00 inaudible]).
  • Foreign Languages: If another language is spoken, provide the timestamp range (e.g., Arabic: 0:00 - 0:16).
  • Speaker ID: Label by name or appearance (e.g., Man in Black Coat, Lady in Red Dress).
  • Full Asset Coverage: Translate Title, Description, Thumbnail, and all Onscreen Text.

CULTURAL DEPTH: Provide literal meanings and detailed notes for cultural references, metaphors, slurs, slang, and idioms.

P1

Phase 1: Structural Audit

Technical verification of metadata and assets.

  • OST Verification: Check Original Script/Text against source audio.
  • Metadata Check: Verify titles, tags, and description formatting.
  • Language ID: Confirm correct dialect and regional markers.
P2

Phase 2: Linguistic Depth

Human review of sync, tone, and nuances.

Active Audio Listening: Full speech path review for sync and natural flow.

Review Notes: Annotate specific timestamps for any suggested edits.

GOAL: ZERO DEFECT HANDOVER

Final approval for linguistic integrity and formatting excellence.

Content & Accuracy

Linguistic precision and cultural depth.

  • Proofreading: Comment the script if you find any mistakes in English script, if the audio is not clear or if any translation is missing, translator will do rest.
  • Grammar: Zero tolerance for errors. Ensure the entire file is flawless.
  • Cultural Context: Provide literal meanings for metaphors, slang, and idioms. Contextualize native acts.

Visuals & Identification

Metadata verification and speaker tagging.

  • Onscreen Elements: Check onscreen texts, video title, description, and thumbnail for accuracy.
  • Speaker Labeling: Label speakers by name or appearance (e.g., Red Shirt, Grey Trouser).
  • Abbreviations: Ensure all abbreviations are expanded to their full forms.

MANDATORY: HIGHLIGHT CHANGES IN YELLOW

Any changes you make in the document MUST BE HIGHLIGHTED IN YELLOW COLOR so they can be easily identified. Proofread the entire file before final submission.

Final Delivery Protocols

Submission Checklists

Client C1

  • Client Checklist: Fill accurately as provided by the client.
  • No OST SS: Screenshots of OST are not required.
  • Privacy: No translator details to be shared with client.
  • Full Asset Sync: Translate Title, Desc, and Thumbnail and then copy paste.
  • Annotation Format: Origin Text + Language + Context (Mandatory).
  • Timestamp Format: Use hh:mm:ss, 00h:00m:00s strictly.
  • Notes: Provide clear reasons for inaudible/unclear speech.

Client C2

  • Timestamp Format: Use 00:00:00 format.
  • Data Entry: Fill Translator Name and all other template details accurately.
  • Handover: Direct Folder Handover via designated project link.

UNIVERSAL FORMATTING EXCELLENCE

Font Size: 9 Font Type: Arial Rule: Do NOT deviate from the given template structure.

HANDOVER COMPLETION: Once checklists are cleared, move task status to "Delivered" and notify the client via the original channel (WhatsApp/Email) with the delivery link.
